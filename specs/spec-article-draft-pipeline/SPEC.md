# Article Draft Pipeline

## Why

A pain to solve plus an opportunity to capture: the owner wants a publishable technical-article draft in ~10 minutes of personal attention, from raw material already in git (dev logs, specs, READMEs, commit history, BMAD memlogs). The owner's initial workflow idea — human extracts key points, AI reconstructs narrative — spends scarce human minutes on the step AI does better (extraction) and delegates the step that most needs human control (claims and voice). This pipeline inverts that: AI harvests with source pointers, the human contributes judgment through a bounded interview and a verification pass. Distribution, not artifact quality, is the current bottleneck (ratified owner finding); this pipeline is the throughput fix.

## Capabilities

- **CAP-1**
  - **intent:** From named sources, the pipeline builds a fact sheet of candidate claims, results, and numbers where every entry carries a source pointer (file/line, commit, or URL).
  - **success:** Auditing any fact-sheet entry leads to the exact source location; entries without a source do not appear.
- **CAP-2** (amended 2026-07-11 per `docs/interview-architecture.md` D1–D3)
  - **intent:** The pipeline interviews the owner with at most 5 targeted questions covering only what sources cannot answer — surprise, significance, opinion, warnings. Every candidate question is triaged against the harvest output (fact sheet + NEEDS-OWNER list — recommendation generation is a view over harvest output and reads nothing else) into exactly one of three outcomes: **suppressed** (fully covered — never reaches the owner), **recommended** (a groundable, source-pointed candidate answer exists, including recorded owner judgments in sources — presented as the default choice with approve/modify/replace/skip dispositions; NEEDS-OWNER re-raises are always recommended, as confirm/deny of the claim), or **open** (genuinely owner-only knowledge — bullet free-text). Stage 2 writes an interview journal to the run workspace recording each question's outcome, survival rationale, grounding pointers, and disposition.
  - **success:** A run on a real project completes the interview in ≤5 questions and ≤5 minutes; a question fully covered by the fact sheet never reaches the owner and its suppression is journaled with the covering entries; every asked question's survival rationale (topic-absent / needs-owner-reraise / owner-judgment) is attributable from the journal without owner intervention; an approved recommended answer carries its source pointers into the draft's provenance.
- **CAP-3** (amended 2026-07-11 per `docs/harness-architecture.md` D1–D2; amended 2026-07-20, #439)
  - **intent:** The pipeline fills the chosen framework (from SPEC-article-frameworks) to produce a draft with schema-conformant frontmatter, classifying every sentence into one of three provenance classes recorded in a sidecar provenance map: a **sourced claim** carries a fact-sheet or interview pointer; a **derived claim** compresses, combines, or restates ≥2 named sourced claims and inherits their pointers — introducing new causality, significance, evaluation, comparison, intent, or scope makes it inferred; **narration** asserts nothing checkable (falsifiability test) and needs no pointer. Inferred claims — asserting more than sources, interview, or derivation support — carry inline `[VERIFY]` markers as before. **Owner opinion as attributed prose (amended 2026-07-20, #439):** owner opinion the interview elicits — thesis, arc, stakes, beliefs and reversals — may enter the draft as **owner-attributed prose spans** classified `sourced`, carrying a **paragraph-granularity interview (question-id) pointer** rather than a per-sentence pointer; this is the prose-shaped channel for the owner's story, distinct from flattening it into atomic sourced claims. The falsifiability contract is unchanged — such prose asserts nothing source-checkable and must not — so it is compatible with the narration rule while remaining explicitly attributed; `verify-provenance` accepts a paragraph-granularity question-id pointer as a valid `sourced` attribution for the span it covers.
  - **success:** For a produced draft, every claim-classed sentence traces to a source pointer, an interview answer, inherited pointers, or a `[VERIFY]` marker; an independent `verify-provenance` check finds no narration-classed sentence asserting a checkable proposition and no derived claim exceeding its inputs; frontmatter passes build validation (AC-4).
- **CAP-4** (amended 2026-07-18, #361/#362 — the variant stage is not part of the draft flow)
  - **intent:** Platform-ready variants — a dev.to copy (full text, `canonical_url` placeholder) and/or a Zenn repo-sync copy (Zenn frontmatter), per the article's language and canonical policy (AP-6) — are produced by a **separate post-review invocation** that consumes the **persisted, reviewed canonical** at `output.drafts`, never a run-workspace intermediate. The draft flow itself ends by durably writing its two declared products (canonical + plan); it offers no platform decision.
  - **success:** Each variant is publishable on its platform without manual reformatting beyond filling the canonical URL; a completed draft run leaves `drafts/{slug}.md` and `plans/{slug}.md` persisted with no platform variant emitted; grepping the draft-flow stages for a platform decision point finds none.
- **CAP-5** (added 2026-07-10)
  - **intent:** Before any generation or review work, the pipeline validates the resolved configuration (`user-config.yaml`, `writing-sources.yaml`): unresolved placeholder values (example-file defaults such as the pointer-block site name), malformed URLs (e.g. double slashes in `canonical_url` composition), and missing required keys are reported as configuration errors up front — publishing configuration is a precondition, never an article-quality finding discovered at the end of the pipeline.
  - **success:** Running the pipeline against a config still carrying an example placeholder or a malformed URL halts at stage 0 with a per-key report naming the file and fix; a clean config produces no configuration findings anywhere later in the run.
- **CAP-6** (added 2026-07-10; amended 2026-07-15, #226)
  - **intent:** Every run — including standalone harvest — ends with a completion summary partitioned into exactly three labeled buckets (**informational notes**, **publish blockers** — e.g. unresolved `[VERIFY]` markers, unrendered figures, a quality-gate failure surviving its revision cycles (CAP-7), **optional cleanup**) followed by an explicit next step. No run ends by only reporting results. **Interaction contract (#226):** every human decision point — the next step included — is presented **in-conversation** (a selection UI where available: e.g. after a standalone harvest, "continue into draft-article / stop here", drafted from the fact-sheet and NEEDS-OWNER counts); local artifact paths (fact sheet, run workspace, logs) may be **displayed informationally** — a copy-pasteable path remains required output — but opening or navigating a local artifact is never a prerequisite for continuing the workflow. A **resumed or partially-completed run** (durability constraint above) reports its last completed stage and the resume path under informational notes, so partial progress is never a silent loss. The **reading-time estimate** (words ÷ ~200 wpm EN, characters ÷ ~500 cpm JA) is an informational-bucket item **only for runs that produce or review an article body**; standalone harvest has no article body to measure and omits it.
  - **success:** A run with a known blocker lists it under publish blockers and nowhere else; every run's summary — harvest included — contains the three bucket headings and a concrete next step presented as an in-conversation choice, with no step that requires the owner to open a machine-state artifact to proceed (paths still printed for reference); an article-producing or article-review run additionally shows a reading-time estimate, while a standalone harvest run shows none.
- **CAP-7** (added 2026-07-11 per `docs/harness-architecture.md` D3–D5)
  - **intent:** A mandatory article-quality gate sits between stage 3 and stage 4 — a stage-progression precondition like the `[VERIFY]` exit criterion, never an advisory finding. The draft must pass a fixed four-dimension rubric (narrative arc, paragraph flow, explanation calibration, readability mechanics; the rubric is a versioned plugin asset), judged by zero-token mechanical checks (dimensions 3 and 4 — dimension 3 amended 2026-07-17, #305: a deterministic vocabulary scan against the rubric's written introduction contract, emitting the complete violation set in one verdict, with audience-known terms excluded via the per-run owner-ratified allowlist) plus one single-pass cheap-tier rubric judge (dimensions 1–2 as the gate; it may emit dimension-3 findings as advisories only, never a gate verdict), **run in a subagent that never saw the drafting turn** (the same isolation NFR13 sets for `verify-provenance`, so the drafting context never grades its own rubric pass), emitting pass/fail per dimension with failing locations, in findings format, no rewritten text. On failure, stage 3 revises against the named failing dimensions and re-runs **both** the quality gate and `verify-provenance` — at most two revision cycles; a failure surviving them surfaces as a publish blocker (CAP-6) naming the failing dimensions and locations, never silently retried or waived. **The second cycle's re-run is a bounded delta re-check, not a fresh full-artifact re-judge (added 2026-07-18, #349): the mechanical dimensions (3–4) re-run in full — they are deterministic and cannot drift — but the cheap-tier dim1–2 judge is scoped to VERIFY that cycle-1's failing locations were addressed; it may not introduce a new interpretive dim1–2 finding, so a genuinely new problem is actionable on the second cycle only when a mechanical dimension flags it. Isolation (NFR13) is preserved: the second-cycle judge still never saw the drafting turn — it receives cycle-1's failing LOCATIONS as its scope, never prior verdicts as "context to be better informed by". And splitting a sentence to satisfy dim4 (readability mechanics) is not scored as a dim1–2 regression — the rubric resolves that conflict, so the two cycles converge instead of oscillating.**
  - **success:** A fact-sheet-stitched draft (wall-to-wall sourced claims with no derived/narration tissue) fails the gate and never reaches stage 4 unrevised; a gate failure after two revision cycles appears in the completion summary's publish-blocker bucket with dimensions and locations; a passing draft enters stage 4 having consumed zero minutes of the owner's attention budget on quality retries.
- **CAP-8** (added 2026-07-20, #432 — per the ratified depth-over-count policy, owner decision record 2026-07-20)
  - **intent:** Article scope, depth, and density are **owner editorial intent set per artifact, never a tool default**. A run may carry an optional **depth/scope directive** captured at stage 0 — or, absent one there, offered as a stage-2 interview item — either a level (`deep-dive | standard | note`) or an explicit one-line scope statement. **Stage-3 fill consumes it**: the directive governs how much each slot elaborates and how many story elements the draft carries — it is **not** a word count or reading-time target. **Reading-time-driven splitting is demoted to a declinable suggestion:** the reading-time estimate stays an informational-bucket item (CAP-6) and drives no split, and any framework's count/length split guidance (e.g. a lessons framework's ">N → split") is a **suggestion the owner may decline**, surfaced as an in-conversation choice under the proposal contract, never an automatic cut. With **no directive**, the run behaves exactly as before this capability (the framework's own defaults), so the directive is an enhancer, never a new required gate.
  - **success:** A run given `depth: deep-dive` produces one deeper article instead of splitting into several; a framework's split hint surfaces as an owner choice ("this would be ~N lessons — one deep-dive, or split?"), never an automatic split; a run with no directive is byte-for-byte the behavior before CAP-8; the reading-time estimate remains informational and drives no split.

## Constraints

- Human attention budget ≤10 minutes per article (interview + verification passes); wall-clock time is unconstrained.
- Durability under a turn ceiling (added 2026-07-12; **resumption made automatic 2026-07-13** per #142): wall-clock is unconstrained but the turn/compute budget is not — the pipeline persists per-stage checkpoint state to the run workspace (`$WS`, building on the intermediates invariant below) and resumes from the last completed stage, so a turn-ceiling casualty is recoverable rather than a total loss. **Resumption is automatic, not opt-in**: on invocation the pipeline detects an in-progress run for the workspace and continues from the last checkpoint without the agent choosing to resume, and a large draft completing across multiple invocations is the normal completion model — a single realistic multi-source input is not required to finish within one turn budget. **The checkpoint unit is sub-stage for the long stages (amended 2026-07-18, #388)**: harvest checkpoints per pinned-source batch and stage 3 per filled section, and resume continues **mid-stage** from the last persisted boundary — a stage whose full cost exceeds one invocation budget still converges across invocations instead of replaying from its start. Each stage surfaces a budget-triage signal before hard failure, and that signal is an **orderly stop, not an advisory**: on breach the stage persists at the next sub-stage boundary, records the partial-progress note (CAP-6), and exits clean — `error_max_turns` or a wall-timeout is a defect of the stop mechanism, never the normal end of an over-budget run.
- Per-stage round-trip economy (added 2026-07-13 per #142): the turn/compute cost of a stage is a first-class constraint — a stage folds its mechanical checks into as few script invocations as its contract allows (e.g. config validation, path resolution, and source enumeration are not separate round-trips when one can carry the others), so a realistic multi-source run makes measurable progress per turn rather than exhausting the budget on orchestration overhead before any reviewable output exists.
- Validator convergence (added 2026-07-15 per #206): an agent-facing mechanical validator must make acceptance reachable without trial-and-error — for every rejectable form there is a sanctioned tooling path that **emits** the accepted form (e.g. the pin helper emits entry-ready `CLAIM / SOURCE / KIND` lines carrying the pinned pointer and the verbatim source text, so quotes and pointers are copied from tool output, never re-typed), and the stage builds entries through that path inside the writing loop, making the validator run a confirmation pass rather than a search procedure. A repair loop against a mechanical validator is bounded at **two passes**; a breach surfaces as the stage's budget-triage signal — never further loops — and entries still rejected route to NEEDS-OWNER per the existing partition rule.
- No invented evidence (amended 2026-07-11): sourced claims carry pointers; derived claims may compress, combine, or restate named fact-sheet inputs — inheriting their pointers — but must not introduce new causality, significance, evaluation, comparison, intent, or scope; narration asserts nothing checkable (falsifiability test: no reviewer with source access could mark it false); every other assertion carries `[VERIFY]`. The pipeline never silently asserts, and the drafting agent never grades its own claim/narration boundary — `verify-provenance` runs independent of the drafting context, **in a subagent that never saw the drafting turn** (NFR13; the same isolation the CAP-7 rubric judge inherits).
- A section needing more than one rewrite routes back to a new interview question, never into open-ended editing; quality-gate revisions (CAP-7) are likewise bounded — two cycles, then a publish blocker, never open-ended iteration. The second cycle is a **bounded delta re-check** that cannot introduce a new interpretive dim1–2 judge finding (#349), so the two cycles converge rather than oscillate on freshly-interpreted findings.
- Missing-input repair route (added 2026-07-18, #348): a review or quality-gate finding classified **missing-input** — an evidence gap the draft cannot fix in prose (insufficient evidence, a missing example/episode, an unsupported narrative claim), as distinct from a writing-problem (structure/prose) finding — may route back **one bounded hop** to a **scoped re-harvest** (a narrowed source set) **or a single bounded owner-elicitation question**, then re-enter the pipeline with the new material. This is the ONLY backward edge to harvest/interview beyond the rewrite route above, and it is governed by the **same bound**: it counts against the same two-cycle limit, and an unrepaired missing-input finding becomes a **publish blocker** rather than a third hop — never open-ended re-harvest. The classification of a review finding as writing-problem vs missing-input is SPEC-article-review's contract; the pipeline never silently re-harvests, and each hop respects the ≤10-minute owner-attention budget (a re-harvest is scoped and pinned; an elicitation is one owner-facing question under the proposal contract). Evidence gathered on a hop is harvested and pinned exactly like any Stage-1 fact — a policy line never becomes a SOURCE, and the no-invented-evidence rule is unchanged.
- Per-section minimum evidence type (added 2026-07-19, #413/#414): at the stage 3→4 boundary the pipeline checks each filled section against the minimum evidence TYPE its framework declares (`episode | example | measurement | none` — the declaration is SPEC-article-frameworks' contract, authored with the templates) as a **stage-progression precondition beside CAP-7**. A section whose declared type is absent is **never silently backfilled with unrelated factual material and never reported as success**: it classifies as a **missing-input** finding and takes the repair route above, under the same two-cycle bound. On that hop, the pipeline constructs candidate **episodes** from already-captured event facts — grouped events plus a one-line narrative frame plus the constituent source pointers; harvest itself stays capture-only and gains no extraction logic — and presents them for **owner selection** as the hop's single bounded elicitation question; a selected episode enters the fact sheet pinned like any harvested material. An unrepaired evidence-type absence surfaces as a **publish blocker naming the section and the missing type** (CAP-6).
- Interview questions (CAP-2) and owner-verification items obey SPEC-writing-assistant's **owner-facing proposal contract**: each shows where the item lands in the article (outline context + short section preview), why it is asked, and choices stating their concrete effect on the article — the owner answers from repository knowledge alone, never by inferring the generation logic.
- Policy seeding (added 2026-07-14, SPEC-policy-source-seam): the optional `policy_source` read seeds candidate **questions only** — it never supplies, pre-fills, or recommends an answer to a factual gap, and triage/recommendation generation remain a view over harvest output; the single bounded exception (added 2026-07-18, SPEC-policy-editorial-direction CAP-6) is a **recommended default for an editorial-judgment gap** (`opinion`/`significance`/`surprise`/`tradeoff`/`warning`/`audience`): recalled from the policy surface and owner-ratified (approve/modify/replace/skip), it counts against the ≤5 cap, keeps `owner_answer` empty at generation, and never lets a policy pointer become a SOURCE — an ineligible-class or tension-item default is a named validator rejection; seeded questions share the ≤5 budget (they displace, never extend it), and **one slot is reserved** (amended 2026-07-17, #302): when at least one valid policy-seeded tension item survives validation, the highest-priority one is guaranteed a slot — it displaces the lowest-priority survivor, never extends the cap — so the editorial anchor (SPEC-policy-editorial-direction CAP-2) and the staging-candidate emitter (SPEC-policy-source-seam CAP-4) are never silently starved by a long NEEDS-OWNER list; a missing or unusable policy source degrades to the generic interview with at most one logged line — never a failure.
- Interview `skip` is captured as a disposition only (added 2026-07-11, `docs/interview-architecture.md` D2): its effect on the target slot — omission, deferral, accepting the recommendation later, `[VERIFY]`-marked inference, or a publish blocker — is the framework slot's declared contract (SPEC-article-frameworks), never an interview-engine default; the skip choice's label states that slot's declared effect.
- Consumes the framework templates from SPEC-article-frameworks verbatim; category structure is not redesigned per run.
- Implemented as a Claude Code skill (`.claude/skills/`) so harvest can read the repo directly.
- All intermediates — fact sheet, NEEDS-OWNER list, interview answers, provenance map, quality-gate judge output, scratch — are written to the run's workspace resolved by the path resolver (`docs/storage-architecture.md` D1–D2), never into the host repository's working tree; the location is a stated contract, not an agent default. Only declared products land in the host repo, at `output.drafts` (SPEC-writing-assistant footprint invariant). **The declared products are two (amended 2026-07-18, #361/#362): the canonical draft `drafts/{slug}.md` and the article plan `plans/{slug}.md` — the draft flow persists both before reporting completion, and a failed write of either is a hard error (the run never reports "complete" with a workspace-only canonical). Platform variants are not draft-flow products; they are emitted by the separate post-review variant invocation (CAP-4).**

## Non-goals

- No publishing: the pipeline never calls dev.to/Zenn APIs or pushes to a Zenn-synced repo; the owner publishes.
- No review: quality passes are SPEC-article-review's contract.
- No image *rendering* or hosting; evidence figures and rendered images are produced by the owner's tooling. *(Narrowed 2026-07-10 by SPEC-article-visuals: proposing visual **source** — Mermaid, figure specs, image-generation prompts — is that spec's contract and in scope for the pipeline; rendering remains out.)*
- No topic selection: the owner chooses what to write; the pipeline does not maintain an idea backlog.

## Success signal

The owner takes a real project (e.g. a QuantScenarioBench release) from "invoke pipeline" to a draft they would hand to review in ≤10 minutes of their own attention, and spot-checking the draft finds zero unmarked invented claims.

## Assumptions

- Drafting runs on the strongest model available to the owner (ratified owner decision: draft quality determines the number of downstream review cycles).
